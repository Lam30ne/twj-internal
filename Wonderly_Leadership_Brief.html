2026 Org Evolution
To the team

We've been building this business the way most small studios do: by doing everything ourselves, learning as we go, and figuring out structure after the fact. That approach got us here — to a team that genuinely cares about the work, clients who trust us, and a studio that has developed a real point of view. We're proud of that.

But we're at a moment where staying in that mode starts working against us. To grow — and to build the kind of place where each of you can genuinely develop — we need to be more intentional about who does what, why, and what we can expect from each other. That's what this document is about.

This isn't a reorganization memo. It's an honest conversation about how we see our own roles evolving, what that means for how you'll experience leadership here, and what we genuinely need from you as this studio becomes what it's capable of becoming.

We're not handing off and stepping back. We're handing off so we can step into the work that moves the whole thing forward — and so you can step into yours.

Adam + Allie Co-Founders, Wonderly / The Wonder Jam
The Direction

Where We're Headed

The growth plan isn't a number on a slide. It's a description of what this studio wants to become — and why.

The goal is $3M in revenue by 2029. But more importantly, it's a studio with fewer clients at higher value, where the work we do is deep and the relationships are real. Where the team has meaningful ownership over their craft, their contribution, and eventually their stake in what we've built. Where the people who've helped us grow can actually benefit from that growth.

The path there runs through three things: pricing what we're worth, deepening the relationships that matter, and building a team that doesn't need us in every room. That last one is the hardest, and it's the most important. It's also what this document is really about.

"The business has always been an organic expression of our skills. Now we're ready to specialize — so the team can grow, and so the work can become what it's actually capable of."

Here's the shape of where we're going, briefly:

Now — Q2 2026
Formalizing what already works

Two delivery tracks. Named discipline leads. Scorecards. Clean financial data. Brief templates. We're naming what's already true and building the infrastructure to sustain it.

Q3 – Q4 2026
Opening the books and hitting stride

The team sees the financial picture. Scorecards are live. First $125K month. 40% growth — not from more clients, but from better ones at better prices.

2027
First profit bonuses and $2M

When the company wins, you win — literally. First quarterly profit pool paid out. VTO target hit. New hires contributing. The machine runs with more independence.

2028 – 2029
$3M, profit sharing, and ownership paths

Annual profit sharing. Equity conversations for senior team members. And the foundation in place for The Wonder Jam's next chapter — built by a team that helped create it.

Allie's Focus

Allie: The Heart and the Standard

Allie is the creative soul of Wonderly. She's staying here, building deeper, and the studio's long-term identity lives through her work.

Allie's background is the through-line of everything Wonderly stands for. Fine arts foundation. Deep technical empathy from learning servers and WordPress before it was fashionable. A decade of listening to what makes personality-driven brands unmistakably themselves. That combination — creative conviction rooted in how things actually work — is rare. It is the reason clients trust us with their brand.

Her role is evolving in a specific direction: less execution, more direction. Not because the execution isn't important — it's everything — but because Allie's judgment, taste, and voice create the most value when they're setting the standard rather than doing the work directly. Taylor, Maria, and Laura do extraordinary work. That work becomes a Wonderly product when Allie's sensibility is the filter it passes through.

Alongside her creative direction role, Allie leads our creator-track client relationships — the chefs, authors, podcasters, and personal brands who hire Wonderly because they want a partner who is genuinely fascinated by what they're building. That fascination is Allie. It can't be replicated by a process or a template.

She is also building a public voice — through Huge Fan, through speaking, through the way she thinks about fandom and owned platforms — that is itself one of Wonderly's most valuable long-term assets. Creator clients find us because they've encountered Allie's ideas. That work deserves protected time and real investment, not whatever hours are left over.

"Deep listening is not just a studio behavior. It's a business strategy. The brands worth building are the ones where the relationship is reciprocal — where we're genuinely curious about the client, and they can feel it."

Adam's Focus

Adam: The Builder Behind the Builder

Adam's focus is shifting toward the infrastructure that makes Wonderly's growth possible — and toward laying the foundation for what comes after.

Adam's long-term trajectory is different from Allie's, and it's worth being honest about that. Allie is building deeper roots at Wonderly. Adam is building the machine that lets Wonderly grow — and eventually, the foundation for The Wonder Jam's next business.

That's not a departure. It's a commitment to making this studio excellent enough that it can sustain and thrive without Adam in every room. The org structure, the financial model, the scorecard system, the hiring roadmap — this is Adam's work right now. Not because he's stepping away from the creative or the clients, but because the highest-leverage thing he can do for this team and this studio is build the infrastructure that makes everyone's work more possible.

In practical terms, this means Adam is focused on business architecture and strategy, team and leadership development, financial stewardship, and the early thinking about what The Wonder Jam's next chapter looks like. Camila is his operational partner. Marin, Matt, Taylor, and Tori are the leaders he's investing in developing. The goal is a studio that doesn't require Adam to be the answer to every question.

The Wonder Jam has always been described as a "family of businesses" — a common substrate that allows specialization to sprout and thrive. Adam's longer arc is building more of that family. Wonderly gets there first. The next one follows from a foundation Wonderly helps create.

Your Right to Demand

What You Can Hold Us To

This is not a list of promises. It's a set of standards we're asking you to actively enforce. If we're not delivering these things, tell us.

What to demand from Allie
Creative Director · Creator Track Lead · External Voice
A clear creative standard. You should always know what "Wonderly quality" means. If you're unsure whether something is on-brand or on-standard, that's a failure of creative direction — not a failure of your judgment. Ask Allie to make it clearer.
Ask directly
Feedback that builds your craft. Allie's role is not just to approve or reject — it's to help you see what she sees. If you're getting feedback without understanding why, ask for the reasoning. That's how taste develops.
Ask for the why
Presence in the creative decisions that matter. Allie should be in the rooms where creative direction is being set — not debugging a timeline or coordinating logistics. If she's caught in operational work, flag it. That's a sign the support structure needs adjusting.
Protect her focus
Honest signal on creator client relationships. Allie leads these relationships. If a creator client is confused about what they're getting, or if the brief doesn't reflect what the client actually needs, Allie needs to know. Don't filter that upward — surface it directly.
Bring problems early
A voice that represents us publicly. Allie's writing and speaking is part of what makes Wonderly findable and credible to the clients we want. If you have ideas for what she should be writing about, or stories from your work that would make great content — share them.
Feed the creative
What to demand from Adam
Business Lead · Org Architect · Leadership Developer
Clarity on the plan and where you fit in it. You should never be guessing about what the business is trying to do or how your role connects to it. If the direction isn't clear, ask Adam to make it specific. Vague strategy is a leadership failure.
Ask for the map
Deliberate investment in your development. Adam's job includes building leaders, not just managing deliverables. If you want to grow into a bigger role, say so explicitly. If you're being underutilized or not being developed, that's worth raising.
Name your ambition
Transparent access to the business picture. The plan is to open the books and share the financial reality with the full team. If the numbers aren't being shared, hold us to that commitment. Understanding how the business works makes your work better.
Ask to see the numbers
Fast decisions on the things that block you. If a structural issue, a resource constraint, or an unclear responsibility is slowing down your work, Adam should resolve it quickly. Surface blockers directly — don't work around them indefinitely.
Escalate blockers
A seat at the table when the org evolves. Changes to structure, roles, or process should be informed by the people doing the work. If a change is being made that affects how you work and you haven't been asked, say something. Good org design requires ground truth.

The Commitment

What We Owe Each Other

Allie has a phrase she comes back to: fandom should feel reciprocal rather than extractive. We think about the team the same way. The relationship between leadership and the people who do the work only holds if it actually goes both directions.

You bring your craft, your care, your honesty, and your willingness to grow. In return, we commit to transparency about where the business is going, deliberate investment in your development, clear roles with real authority, and eventually, meaningful financial participation in what we build together. The profit bonus, the profit sharing, the equity conversations — those aren't distant hypotheticals. They're the reason the org work matters right now. We're building the infrastructure that makes those things possible.

What we love about this studio — and we genuinely do love it — is that it was built around real relationships, real craft, and a real point of view. Growing it doesn't mean losing that. It means building the systems that protect it as more people join, as more clients trust us, and as the work gets bigger and more complex.

The path to $3M is not a financial plan. It is a picture of a studio where the work is excellent, the relationships are deep, the team is thriving, and the business is honest about what it is and where it's going. That is what we are building. And we are building it with you.

"Clear process makes creativity effortless. That's true of design. It's just as true of how a leadership team works with the people around them."

"Don't just rent space online." We built a studio on that belief. Now we're building a team on it — people who genuinely own their work, their growth, and their contribution to something lasting.
