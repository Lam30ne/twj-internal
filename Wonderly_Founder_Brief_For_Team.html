Wonderly · Internal Team Brief · March 2026

Where We're Going —
and What That Means for You

A note from Adam and Allie on how we're each showing up differently as Wonderly grows — and what we need from each other to get there.

From
Adam + Allie Lehman · Co-founders, Wonderly

We've been building this together for a long time. And one of the things that's made us good at it is that we've both been willing to do whatever the business needed — design, sales, operations, client calls, admin, strategy, all of it.

That willingness got us here. But it's also the thing that, if we don't address it intentionally, will keep us from getting to where we're going. When both founders are everywhere, no one owns anything fully. And the team deserves leaders who are fully present in a defined place — not spread across every corner of the business.

So this is a note about something we're doing on purpose: specializing. It's not that our work changes dramatically overnight. It's that we're being deliberate about where each of us puts our best energy, and we're being honest with you about what that means for how you work with us.

We're sharing this because we believe the team deserves to know what to expect — and what to ask for — from the people leading this business. Clear process makes creativity effortless. That applies to leadership too.

With love and intention — Adam + Allie
The big picture

What We're Building
and Why It Matters

Wonderly is on a path to $3M — not as a vanity milestone, but because it represents something real: a business that can invest in A+ talent, pay people well, share its profits with the team, and deliver a level of work that none of us could do alone. The numbers and the org structure are being designed to get us there, but numbers don't move on their own. People do.

Here's the honest version of what's happening: we're growing up as a business. We're transitioning from a studio where the founders handle everything to a company where a real leadership team owns real domains. Two client tracks, three discipline leads, a Chief of Staff running the back of house. The pieces are in place or being put in place. And the founders are specializing along with everyone else.

Now through Q3 2026
Getting the structure right

Org clarity, financial visibility, brief discipline, scorecard rollout. Building the systems that let everyone do their best work with less friction.

Q4 2026
First $400K quarter

Fewer clients at higher value. Pricing that reflects what we actually deliver. Both tracks running with confidence.

2027
$2M and the first team profit bonus

When the company wins, you win. The transparency roadmap opens up the books, and the bonus structure makes that real.

2029
$3M — and what comes next

A team that can hold Wonderly without both founders in every room. Profit sharing, equity conversations, and the next business in The Wonder Jam's family of businesses beginning to take shape.

Why we're telling you this: because the path to $3M runs directly through you. It runs through Marin growing into an elite account manager. Through Matt being fully unleashed as a dev process lead. Through Taylor, Tori, and the design team doing the best work of their careers. Through Camila building the operational infrastructure that makes all of it visible and sustainable. This is not a founders-lead-everything story. It's a team story.

Allie's role

Allie Is the
Heart and Voice of Wonderly

Wonderly exists because Allie has a point of view — about what great creative work looks like, about what it means for a brand to truly own its relationship with its audience, about what the internet should be for the people and communities it serves. That point of view is not a marketing asset. It is the business.

The clients we do our most meaningful work with — the chefs, the authors, the podcasters, the personality-driven brands — they are not buying a website. They are buying access to Allie's sensibility, her philosophy, her deep listening. That's rare and it compounds over time. It should be protected.

What that means practically is that Allie is shifting away from day-to-day delivery and toward the work that only she can do. Taylor, Maria, and Laura execute within the standard Allie's aesthetic defines. The project coordinator role being added to the creator track exists specifically to absorb the operational weight so Allie can hold relationships at the level they deserve.

What Allie is moving toward

Where Allie's energy goes
What that looks like in practice
Creative direction and the quality standard
Allie sets the aesthetic bar for what leaves the studio. She reviews the work that matters most. She defines what "Wonderly quality" means — Taylor, Maria, and Laura execute within that standard.
Creator client relationships
The dozen or so creator client relationships that are at the heart of Wonderly's track record. These clients want Allie's judgment and perspective. She's deepening those, not managing timelines on them.
External voice and thought leadership
Huge Fan, Offline Recess, speaking, panels — this is not a side project. It is one of Wonderly's most powerful business development tools. The creator world follows people, not brands. Allie writing and speaking with authority is how the next generation of creator clients finds us.
Deep listening as a leadership practice
Allie brings her listening practice to the team too — being present to what's working and what isn't, what people need to do their best work, what the studio needs to stay true to itself as it grows.

What this means for you: If you're on the design team, you should expect clear creative direction from Allie — not day-to-day project management. Bring her the work that needs creative judgment. Don't wait for her to track your Harvest hours. If you're on the creator track, Allie is genuinely available for the relationship moments that matter. Use that access intentionally.

Adam's role

Adam Is Building
the Machine That Scales

Adam's job is to build a company that can hold itself — and then build the next one. That's not an abstract ambition. It's a specific, practical set of responsibilities that require sustained focus: the org structure, the financial visibility, the leadership development, the growth decisions, and eventually the new business that will emerge from The Wonder Jam's family of businesses.

The truth is that as long as Adam is deep in client delivery and day-to-day operations, those things get squeezed out. And they're the things that, if done well, are worth more to the business than any single project. So Adam is moving his energy toward the work that multiplies everyone else's capacity — not just adding to his own.

What Adam is moving toward

Where Adam's energy goes
What that looks like in practice
Business architecture and strategy
The org structure, the two-track model, the hiring roadmap, the financial targets. Adam holds the long-range view of what Wonderly is becoming and makes the structural decisions that shape it.
Team and leadership development
The path to $3M runs through Marin, Matt, Taylor, Tori, and Camila growing into their full potential. Adam's investment in developing this leadership team is the investment in eventually stepping back — and the most important thing he can do with his time.
Financial stewardship
Pricing decisions, client mix, cash position, the budget, the growth levers. Adam is in the numbers so the business decisions are grounded in reality — not instinct alone.
Business development — non-creator track
Alongside Marin, Adam is helping build the pipeline and client relationships on the non-creator track — the organizational clients where Allie isn't needed in the room and the business can scale most independently.
The next chapter of The Wonder Jam
The Wonder Jam is a family of businesses. Wonderly is the core. But the long vision includes additional businesses born from this team's expertise. Adam is beginning to think about what comes next — while making sure Wonderly doesn't need him to exist.

What this means for you: Adam is your resource for business clarity — strategy questions, structural decisions, growth-related concerns. Bring him real problems that need real thinking. Don't wait for him to check in on your daily tasks. If you're on the leadership team, expect direct investment in your development — and expect to be held to clear standards in return.

The other direction

What We're
Asking From You

  • Bring Allie the work that needs creative direction, not the daily updates

Matt + Alyssa + Jerrod + Dev Team

Lucy + Andrea · Admin Team

Our promise to you

What We Commit
to in Return

"When the company wins, you win. That's not a slogan — it's the structure we're building toward."

We are working toward a business where the team understands the numbers, shares in the profits, and has a real stake in what we're building together. That's the Zingerman's model we've been inspired by — open books, shared wins, and eventually equity conversations with the people who helped build this. Here is what we commit to as we get there.

What we commit to doing
Why it matters
Open the books by Q3 2026
You will see revenue, gross margin, and net income. Not individual salaries — the business metrics. Because a team that understands the business can make better decisions inside it.
Publish scorecards for every role
No one should wonder if they're doing a good job. Clear metrics, honest feedback, real signals — so you know where you stand and what excellent looks like.
Pay a team bonus when the company hits its targets
First bonus in Q1 2027 if we hit our numbers. Tied to company performance, paid to everyone. The point is the signal: we win together.
Invest in your growth, not just your output
We want Marin to become elite. We want Matt to be fully unleashed. We want Taylor, Tori, and Alyssa to grow into the leaders this business needs. That requires investment, not just expectation.
Protect maker time and calm execution
A happy, calm creative team makes better work. Allie built Wonderly around that belief. We're building processes, briefing systems, and scheduling norms that protect focus — yours and ours.
Tell you the truth, even when it's hard
Clear feedback, honest numbers, direct communication about what's working and what isn't. We'd rather have an uncomfortable conversation early than a bigger problem later.

That line is about our clients. But it applies here too. We are not renting your talent — showing up while it's convenient and moving on when the next thing comes. We are building something durable, together, that is meant to last.

The same philosophy applies inside this business. You deserve a place to do your best work, to grow into your full capacity, and to share in what we build.

The specialization happening at the founder level is not about us doing less. It is about all of us doing more of what we're actually here to do. Allie building the creative standard and the external voice that makes Wonderly Wonderly. Adam building the company that can hold all of us and eventually build what comes next. And each of you owning your domain with the kind of clarity and confidence that makes great work possible.

The network is already alive. We're just making it visible to itself.

With deep appreciation for what you bring — Adam + Allie
