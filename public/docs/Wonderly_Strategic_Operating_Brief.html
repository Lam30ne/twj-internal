Brief Frameworks

No Figma opens. No dev environment spins up. No Nakul gets briefed. Until a brief is submitted and approved by the discipline lead. These are the minimum required fields.

Design Brief
Required before any design work begins · Owner: Taylor
Client + Project ✱
Client name, project name, track (Creator / Non-Creator), account lead.
e.g. Aunt Flow · Retainer Q2 Campaign · Creator Track · Allie
Deliverable ✱
Exactly what is being designed. Format, dimensions, quantity. No vague categories.
e.g. 3 email headers (600px wide) + 1 social cover (1200×630) + homepage hero refresh (desktop + mobile)
Goal ✱
What is this design trying to accomplish? Not aesthetic — business or campaign goal.
e.g. Drive email sign-ups for new product launch. Not "look good."
Audience ✱
Who is seeing this? Age, context, platform, relationship to the brand.
Brand Reference ✱
Link to brand guide, existing assets, approved color palette, fonts. Taylor does not go hunting for these.
Tone + Direction
3–5 adjectives. Optional inspiration references or "do not do" examples.
e.g. Warm, editorial, premium. Not: corporate, busy, or neon.
Copy / Content ✱
All text that will appear in the design. If copy isn't ready, design doesn't start.
Hours Estimate ✱
Account lead or Taylor provides an hour estimate before design opens. Actual hours tracked in Harvest against this estimate.
e.g. Estimated: 6 hours. Triggers change order if exceeded by >20%.
Due Date ✱
Hard client-facing deadline. Taylor sets internal deadline 2 days prior for review buffer.
Revision Rounds Included
State how many rounds are included per contract. Default: 2. Any additional rounds require account lead approval.
Development Brief
Required before any dev environment opens · Owner: Matt (approved by Alyssa or Jerrod)
Client + Project ✱
Client name, project name, platform (WP or Shopify), account lead, dev lead assigned.
e.g. Citron Hygiene · New Site Build · Shopify · Marin · Jerrod
Scope of Work ✱
Detailed list of what is being built. Every page, integration, feature, and custom element. This is the scope protection document.
e.g. 6-page Shopify build, custom product filter, Klaviyo integration, Google Analytics 4, mobile QA.
Explicitly Out of Scope ✱
List what is not included. This protects against scope creep and enables change orders.
e.g. Blog migration, custom checkout, email design, photography.
Design Status ✱
Where are approved designs? Dev does not start until designs are approved by the client. Link to approved Figma frames.
Integrations / Third-Party ✱
All tools to be connected: CRM, email, analytics, payment, booking, forms. Credentials location.
Hosting + Environment ✱
Who hosts (Valerian? Client? Other)? Staging environment URL. Domain ownership. DNS access confirmed?
Hours Estimate ✱
Matt or lead dev provides hour estimate per deliverable. Tracked in Harvest. >20% overrun triggers account lead review before continuing.
White-Label Involved?
If Nakul's team is involved: define exactly what they're building, who reviews their output, and who communicates changes. Not Nakul directly to client.
QA Checklist ✱
Link to or attach platform-specific QA checklist (WP or Shopify). QA is completed by lead dev before handoff to account lead. Not by client first.
Launch Date ✱
Client-facing go-live date. Internal dev complete date set 5 business days prior for QA and account lead review.

Implementation note: These briefs should live as ClickUp templates — one for design, one for dev. A project cannot move from "Scoping" to "In Progress" in ClickUp until the brief template is completed. Matt owns the dev brief gate. Taylor owns the design brief gate. This is where the discipline of briefs gets enforced without a separate process layer.
