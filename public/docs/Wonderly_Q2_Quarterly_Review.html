03 — Concepts Agreed Upon

What We Believe Now

Ideas that landed and held. These are now operating philosophy — not just meeting observations.

⟷
Two Tracks Are Not a Hierarchy
Track 1 (Creator) and Track 2 (Non-Creator / B2B) are both important. Neither is "better." The team has never been told this explicitly. It must be communicated clearly — the framing matters.
↑
Tracks Scale Independently
As each track grows, its own delivery team and specialization can scale without contaminating the other. The concierge model mirrors this. Lucy's world grows separately from Andrea's world. They're not fishing in the same pond — by design.
⏱
Expedite Fee Model
Track 2 basic membership has a 36-hour SLA. If a client needs same-day response, they can pay an expedite fee. Not just revenue — it reinforces scarcity, sets standards, and removes entitlement thinking from both client and team. The "Goddess example" made it real.
◈
Account Manager Clears the FOH/BOH Channel
The account manager role is the single communication layer between clients and back of house. BOH only enters for specific, scheduled value-creation moments. All service standard communication routes through account management. This is the structural solution to the FOH/BOH friction problem.
₿
Blended Cost Rate Must Be Formalized
True cost of a team member isn't their hourly rate — it's their rate adjusted for what percentage of their time is billable. Allie has been running this manually in her head for years. Camila's unit economics work will make it real data.
↗
Track 2 Revenue Ceiling Is Very High
Adam's belief, stated explicitly: Track 2 structured correctly could be a $10M business. The floor can keep rising — Wonderly doesn't have to chase small B2B work forever. As standards and systems mature, minimum contract size increases.
✦
"Wonderly by Allie" — The Couture Tier
Camila introduced the Prada analogy: Wonderly is the brand, "Wonderly by Allie" is the couture tier. Track 1 creator work could triple in price point as demand and brand positioning strengthens. Adam and Allie both affirmed the direction.
⬡
Founder Content as Strategic Unlock
Adam and Allie giving away strategic thinking publicly — frameworks, playbooks, concepts — is both a marketing play and a way to serve people who can't afford Wonderly yet. Strong marketing in front of the pipeline reduces friction at the sales conversation. If the prospect understands the value before the intro call, price justification is no longer the job.
⚑
The Pattern of Great Calls Followed by Nothing Is Over
Allie named this explicitly as a historical pattern. This meeting felt different. The stated intent: alignment doesn't survive only as alignment — it must survive execution. Camila's role is the structural answer to that risk. This closing sentiment is now a commitment on the record.
On Team Development
Camila's direction to Adam: the team has potential but carries real confidence deficits. Many team members — particularly women on international teams — have been conditioned not to speak their minds. Adam's people-reading ability and genuine care make him well-positioned to build intentional development pathways. The ask: think about who becomes a lead in five years, and architect the conversations, opportunities, and books that get them there.

Bottom Line

The documents are strongest where the concepts are most settled — the two tracks, the brief discipline, the founder roles, the $3M arc. They hold together well as a leadership alignment suite for the things decided before this meeting.

What the April 3 meeting generated is a layer of operational specificity the documents don't yet carry: the expedite fee, the SLA matrix, the Lucy/Andrea formalization, the May handoff timeline, the post-mortem standard, and the explicit deferral of marketing strategy. Those need to make it into the document suite before the next quarterly — or the documents are already operating instructions for a business that no longer matches the decisions made.

The more significant gap is the strategic divergence on Track 2's ceiling ($10M vs. the documents' modest framing) and the "Wonderly by Allie" luxury tier for Track 1. If those ideas hold — and the energy in the meeting suggests they will — the current documents underframe both tracks' long-range potential. That matters for how Camila writes the two-track definition document, which is her first deliverable.

The Urgency Flag
The "Tori hire" and "Pasta Queen repricing" silence in the meeting against their urgency in the documents is the most operationally concerning gap. Both were written as "this week" and "cannot wait for Q3" items. Neither surfaced in a full quarterly strategy session. That's either a sign they've been handled and the docs weren't updated — or a sign they've been drifting. Either answer needs to be confirmed explicitly.
